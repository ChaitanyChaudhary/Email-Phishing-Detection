EMAIL PHISHING DETECTION - ENTITY RELATIONSHIP DIAGRAM (Simple Text Format)
================================================================================

ENTITIES:
---------

1. USERS
   - email (Primary Key)
   - password
   - user_id

2. EMAILS
   - email_id (Primary Key)
   - subject
   - sender
   - body
   - headers
   - received_date
   - is_multipart
   - content_type
   - user_email (Foreign Key to USERS)

3. PHISHING_ANALYSES
   - analysis_id (Primary Key)
   - email_id (Foreign Key to EMAILS)

   - model_version
   - phishing_probability
   - legitimate_probability

   - analysis_timestamp

   - subject
   - body
   - created_date
   - source

6. LOG_RECORDS
   - log_id (Primary Key)
   - user_email (Foreign Key to USERS)
   - action_type
   - message
   - timestamp
   - log_level
   - file_path


RELATIONSHIPS:
--------------

1. USERS to EMAILS
   - One-to-Many relationship
   - One user can receive many emails
   - Relationship: user_email in EMAILS references email in USERS

2. USERS to LOG_RECORDS
   - One-to-Many relationship
   - One user can generate many log records
   - Relationship: user_email in LOG_RECORDS references email in USERS

3. EMAILS to PHISHING_ANALYSES
   - One-to-Many relationship
   - One email can have many analysis results
   - Relationship: email_id in PHISHING_ANALYSES references email_id in EMAILS

4. ML_MODELS to PHISHING_ANALYSES
   - One-to-Many relationship
   - One ML model can be used for many analyses
   - Relationship: model_id in PHISHING_ANALYSES references model_id in ML_MODELS

5. TRAINING_DATA to ML_MODELS
   - One-to-Many relationship
   - Training data is used to train ML models
   - Relationship: Multiple training data records contribute to one model


SUMMARY:
--------

Total Entities: 6
Total Relationships: 5

Primary Entities: USERS, ML_MODELS
Dependent Entities: EMAILS, PHISHING_ANALYSES, LOG_RECORDS, TRAINING_DATA
